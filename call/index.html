Call for Papers

The CMC-Corpora Conference brings together language-centered research on computer-mediated communication (CMC) and social media across linguistics, philologies, communication and media studies, and the social sciences. We particularly welcome work grounded in corpus and computational linguistics, language and text technology, and machine learning.

The conference features research that (i) applies computational methods and tools to the empirical analysis of CMC/social-media phenomena and (ii) builds, processes, annotates, represents, and exploits CMC/social-media corpora, including integration into digital research infrastructures.

We adopt a broad definition of CMC and social media, spanning email, newsgroups, forums, chat and messenger apps (e.g., WhatsApp), social networks (e.g., Facebook, Instagram), gaming platforms, as well as interaction on video portals (e.g., YouTube, TikTok), learning platforms, mobile apps, online games, and virtual worlds.

Topics of Interest

Development of CMC / Social Media Corpora

  • End-to-end corpus creation: from data collection to publication
  • Open-access data for CMC research: ethics and GDPR compliance
  • Annotation of CMC data: genres, linguistic layers, metadata
  • Multimodal corpora
  • Large-scale (“big data”) corpora
  • Legal aspects of sampling, distribution, and long-term archiving

Analysis of CMC / Social Media Corpora

  • Sociolinguistic studies of CMC
  • Discourse-analytic approaches to CMC
  • Linguistic characteristics of CMC
  • Multimodal (incl. visual) analysis
  • Multilingualism and code-switching
  • CMC in language education

Natural Language Processing for CMC / Social Media

  • Text normalization
  • Part-of-speech tagging
  • Anonymization and pseudonymization
  • Lemmatization
  • Syntactic parsing
  • Semantic annotation
  • LLMs for CMC analysis

We welcome submissions addressing any of the above areas or closely related topics.
