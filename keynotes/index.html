Keynote Speakers

Mikko Laitinen

University of Eastern Finland

Mikko Laitinen is Professor of English Language at the University of Eastern Finland. His research explores language variation and change through social network theory and the study of World Englishes, with a focus on Finland and the Nordic region. He specializes in computational and data-intensive approaches to language, combining large-scale social media data with traditional corpora. Laitinen leads major projects on digital humanities infrastructures (FIN-CLARIAH) and on the diffusion of linguistic innovations in online networks (COMET).

Sofia Rüdiger

Technische Universität Berlin

Prof. Dr. Sofia Rüdiger is a Dozent the the Freie Universität Berlin whose research bridges World Englishes, sociolinguistics, and computer-mediated communication. Her work focuses on Asian Englishes, especially those used in South Korea and Taiwan, and explores how multilingualism, language contact, and code-switching shape English in global contexts. She combines corpus-based and ethnographic methods to investigate digital discourse, persuasion, and variation in both spoken and online language.
